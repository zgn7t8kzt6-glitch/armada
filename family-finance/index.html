The Conscious Spending Plan

From Ramit Sethi. You don't need a line-item budget — you need four buckets. Money goes to each bucket automatically, and whatever lands in “guilt-free” you spend without guilt. That's the answer to “I don't know how to spend money”: decide the buckets once, then enjoy the spending bucket on purpose.

Guideline ranges: Fixed costs 50–60% · Investments 10–20% · Savings for goals 5–10% · Guilt-free spending 20–35%. If fixed costs are over 60%, that's the problem to fix — not the coffee.

How much into real estate?

There's no magic number, but there is a sane range. A common target for families like yours: 25–40% of net worth in real estate (including your own home), with the rest spread across index funds, cash reserves, and the business. The mistake isn't owning real estate — it's owning only real estate.

Rules of thumb before any purchase: ① Keep 6 months of expenses in cash first, always. ② A rental should cash-flow from day one — don't count on appreciation. ③ You already own a business; the business is a concentrated asset, so real estate is diversification #2, index funds are diversification #1. ④ Never buy under time pressure. Good deals recur; bad deals demand urgency.

Protecting the kids — the checklist, in order

Do these top to bottom. The first four cost little and cover 90% of the risk. Check them off as you and Rachel get them done.

Straight talk: a trust is a container, not magic. For most families the right first move is a revocable living trust + wills + term life insurance + 529s, done with a real estate-planning attorney (typically $2,500–$6,000 flat fee). Irrevocable trusts, SLATs, and dynasty trusts only matter once your estate approaches the federal exemption territory — that's a “hire the specialist” conversation, not a DIY one.
