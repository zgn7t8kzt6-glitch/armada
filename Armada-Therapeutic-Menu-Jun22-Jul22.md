# Armada Recovery — Therapeutic Menu
### June 22 – July 22, 2026 · Detox & Residential

> Food is one of the most powerful things we do. In early recovery the brain and body are
> depleted and inflamed; the right nutrition reduces cravings, steadies mood, restores sleep,
> repairs the gut, and tells a person — before anyone says a word — *you matter here.*
> This menu is built to do that, three meals a day, for a month.

**⚠️ Clinical note:** Review with your Registered Dietitian / medical team before use. Adjust
for individual allergies, diabetes, renal/hepatic disease, **refeeding-syndrome risk** (common in
severe alcohol/eating-disorder histories — advance calories gradually under medical supervision),
texture needs, and cultural/religious preferences. This is a framework, not a prescription.

---

## 1. The science we're building on

**Substance use depletes the body.** Alcohol strips thiamine (B1), folate, B6, B12, magnesium,
and zinc. Opioids slow the gut (constipation, poor appetite). Stimulants cause weight loss and
broad nutrient loss. Almost everyone arrives dehydrated, blood-sugar-dysregulated, and inflamed.

**Eight priorities drive every meal:**

1. **Protein at every meal** — amino acids rebuild the neurotransmitters addiction burned out
   (tyrosine → dopamine for motivation; tryptophan → serotonin → melatonin for mood & sleep).
   Target ~20–30g protein per meal.
2. **Complex carbohydrates** (oats, whole grains, sweet potato, legumes, fruit) — steady blood
   sugar = fewer cravings and mood crashes; supports serotonin production.
3. **Healthy fats / Omega-3s** (salmon, eggs, walnuts, flax, chia, olive oil, avocado) — brain
   repair, lower inflammation and depression.
4. **B-vitamins & thiamine** (eggs, legumes, leafy greens, fortified whole grains, lean pork) —
   directly replace what alcohol depleted.
5. **Magnesium & zinc** (nuts, seeds, beans, whole grains, dark greens) — calm anxiety, support
   sleep and immune recovery.
6. **Fiber + fermented foods** (produce, legumes, whole grains, yogurt) — fix opioid constipation
   and rebuild the gut–brain axis.
7. **Hydration & electrolytes** all day — water, broth, herbal tea, electrolyte drinks.
8. **Tryptophan-forward dinners** (turkey, fish, dairy, eggs, oats, seeds) + complex carbs in the
   evening to support sleep.

**Three things we limit:** added sugar (mood swings, cravings), excess caffeine (anxiety/insomnia —
especially in detox), and fried/greasy/heavily-processed food (GI distress).

**Rhythm:** 3 meals + 2 snacks, on a predictable schedule. Regular timing alone stabilizes blood
sugar and reduces cravings — and predictability is its own form of care.

## 2. Detox-phase protocol (first ~24–72 hours, or until appetite returns)

In early withdrawal, appetite is low and nausea is common. **Don't push a full tray** — offer small,
bland, easy-to-digest food often, with relentless hydration. For each day below there's a
**"Detox-light"** swap; use it until the client tolerates the full menu, then advance.

- **Start bland & gentle:** oatmeal, plain toast, crackers, bananas, applesauce, rice, scrambled
  eggs, broth-based soups, plain yogurt, smoothies. (BRAT-style, plus protein.)
- **Small & frequent:** half-portions every 2–3 hours beat one big plate.
- **Nausea kit:** ginger tea/ginger chews, peppermint tea, crackers, cold water, popsicles, broth.
- **Hydrate hard:** water + an electrolyte option with every contact; broth between meals.
- **Avoid early:** greasy, spicy, very sweet, heavy/fried foods; caffeine.
- **Advance as tolerated:** bland → soft → full menu, adding protein and color as appetite returns.

---

## 3. Standing all day (every day, both populations)

- **Hydration station:** water (infused with citrus/cucumber/berries), electrolyte drinks, broth,
  **decaf & herbal teas** (ginger, peppermint, chamomile in the evening). Decaf coffee available;
  limit caffeinated coffee to mornings.
- **Snack carts (mid-morning & mid-afternoon):** fresh fruit, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese,
  hummus + veggies, whole-grain crackers + cheese, hard-boiled eggs, mixed nuts/seeds, nut butter
  + apple/banana, protein bites, ginger chews. Always something within reach — *never let someone
  reach for a snack and find it empty.*

## 5. Special-diet swaps (keep these on hand every day)

- **Vegetarian/vegan:** swap the protein for beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, or a veggie
  patty; use plant yogurt/milk. (Beans + whole grain = complete protein.)
- **Gluten-free:** use GF bread/wraps, rice, quinoa, oats (certified), corn tortillas.
- **Dairy-free:** plant yogurt/milk/cheese; nut butters for protein/fat.
- **Diabetic:** emphasize protein + fiber, keep portions of grains/fruit controlled, no added sugar,
  no juice.
- **Soft/dental:** the Detox-light versions double as soft-diet options.

## 6. Why this works (the one-paragraph version for staff)

Every plate has **protein** to rebuild the brain's chemistry, **complex carbs** to steady mood and
kill cravings, **color** for the vitamins and minerals addiction stole, **omega-3s** several nights a
week for repair, and **calming, tryptophan-rich dinners** so people actually sleep. We hydrate
relentlessly, keep caffeine and sugar low, and never let someone reach for food and find none. In
recovery, a warm, thoughtful meal isn't just nutrition — it's the most physical proof we can give
that this person is worth caring for.
