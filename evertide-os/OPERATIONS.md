# EverTide OS — Operations Guide

How EverTide Infusion actually runs on this system, day by day. The
philosophy: Studer-style visible accountability, Schulze-style teachable
standards, defect-log discipline, one DRI per object, and institutional
memory that outlives any meeting.

## Daily (5–10 minutes per person)

**Every morning, open Home.**

1. Read the red banner first. If `OPENING DATE AT RISK` is up, that is the
   day's priority — tap it to see every cause.
2. Work **Waiting on You** to zero: overdue tasks, blocked tasks, due
   commitments, MISSING KPIs, risk reviews, decisions awaiting you. Each row
   links straight to the fix.
3. Update your tasks *as you work them* — status, percent, a one-line
   comment. A task with no meaningful update for 7 days goes **stale** and
   gets called out in the huddle; don't be that row.
4. Hit a wall? Mark the task **Blocked** — the app forces you to say exactly
   what's blocking and who can unblock it. Blocked work is never hidden.
5. Found a defect (something broke, slipped, or embarrassed us)? **Log an
   issue** in under a minute from your phone. High/critical issues route to
   the next huddle automatically. Surfacing problems is a win, not a
   confession.

**Owner habits:** you own every object with your avatar on it. Helpers help;
the owner answers for it. If you truly can't own it, ask an admin to reassign
— silent orphans are not a thing here.

## Weekly cadence

### Monday — scorecard opens
- 7:00 AM: every weekly KPI owner is notified. Enter your number (phone-
  friendly, under a minute) with a one-line narrative. No number by Tuesday
  = **MISSING**, scored red, first item on the huddle agenda with your name
  on it. Never enter a fake zero.

### Tuesday — leadership huddle (the heartbeat)
Before: 8:00 AM cron escalates any still-missing KPIs to owners and admins.
Facilitator creates/opens the huddle and taps **Start** — the agenda freezes:

1. Countdown + opening-risk status
2. **Wins** — celebrate first, always
3. Scorecard, missing/red first — owner speaks to each
4. Critical path & milestones
5. Overdue → Blocked → Stale tasks — owner + unblock plan for each
6. High/critical issues — root cause and corrective action, not blame
7. High/severe risks — mitigation movement
8. **Prior commitments** — every single one goes done / carried / cancelled
   (with reason). Carrying shows `Carried 2x` for a reason: the third carry
   is a leadership conversation, not a shrug.
9. Decisions made in the room get logged in the Decision Log before the room
   empties
10. **New commitments** — who / what / by when, captured live

**End Huddle** refuses to close until every prior commitment is dispositioned,
then freezes the record forever. Miss the huddle? The Tuesday-evening cron
tells the admins — the system never pretends a huddle happened.

### Any day
- Convert an occurred risk to an issue the moment it materializes — the risk
  history stays, the work moves to the defect log.
- New vendor contract? Enter renewal-notice and end dates — the 60-day alert
  does the remembering.

## Monthly cadence

1. The 1st: the monthly report drafts itself (trends, goal/project progress,
   budget & runway from the Financial KPIs, payer/staffing/construction/
   clinical/referral summaries, recurring defects, repeated carryovers,
   decision outcomes due).
2. Leadership reviews the draft in Reports, adds the narrative, **finalizes**
   (immutable), prints/PDFs for the board or lenders.
3. Monthly management review agenda = the report itself: recurring defects
   (same issue linked twice = systemic), commitments carried 2x+, decision
   outcomes due for review (did the call work? record it), goal progress vs.
   the annual goal.
4. Strategy check: update goal progress/status; at-risk goals get an owner
   plan, not a hopeful shrug.

## Documents discipline

One authoritative home per document (§2.11). Upload a **new version** —
never a renamed copy. Restricted documents (equity, comp, legal) are visible
only to admins and explicitly granted people. And the rule that has no
exceptions: **no patient-identifiable information, ever** — patient data
lives in the EMR.

## When something goes wrong

- Something slipped → log an issue, own it, fix it, write the resolution.
  Reopened issues keep their history; recurring ones get linked so patterns
  surface in the monthly report.
- The opening date is genuinely threatened → an admin declares opening risk
  (Admin → Settings) so the banner tells everyone the truth immediately.
- A decision turned out wrong → don't edit history. Supersede it with the
  new decision and record the outcome on the old one. The log is only
  valuable if it never lies.
